Carlmart

Team Members: Colin James, Wesley Yang, Khizar Qureshi

Project Description: Many students at Carleton College have a problem in the final days of their senior year. They find themselves with too many items, necessary for college life, to travel with or have items that they don’t want in their future. Their last days in the dorms are spent throwing lightly used and valuable items in Lighten up, the dumpster, or trying to pawn them off others. Our project will provide a platform that makes it easy for seniors, or Carleton students of any year, to put lightly used and new items onto the site for other Carleton students to buy, barter, or pick up for free.

Description of Figma Prototype: Prototype addresses the user needs I learned about from the Fictional Inquary I performed with the seniors in Carleton. Both seniors mentioned that a filter feature will be very useful for the user as it will allow them to look for desired items much easier. Therfore I included the filter feature at the left side which allows you to filter the price range of assignments and key items that most students may find useful such as furnitures and electronics. Also one of the senior mentioned that at the home page of the store, it should show recommended items for user to take a look at based off of the usesrs search history and items bought. I also added a feature to like/favorite an item in the store which allows you to keep track of items you want to come back for and possibly implement a recommendation algorithm that keeps them track for the recommendation section at the home page.


First requirement: Buy Page. Buy page is a core function of the marketplace that allows the users to browse and purchase items. Without a functional buy page, users cannot make any purchase and engage with the platform meaningfully.
Second requirement: Shopping cart. A shopping cart is essential feature for user convenience, allowing users to collect and review items before purchasing. This allows the user to make a final check of what to buy and how much it will cost. If the user chnages their mind or see a item they never wanted, user can simply remove them.
Third requirement: Sell Page. A functional sell page is critical for sustaining marketplace supply; without sellers listing items, buyers have nothing to purchase. Being able to sell item is key for Carlmart as the whole purpose of Carlmart is to have students not waste products they don't need by trashing them but selling them.

At the navigation bar at the top of the home page, you can click on the "sell" button to open the sell page that allows you to upload the image of your item you want to sell and set a price for it. This prototype addresses the crucial sell page requirement.

The second video is the shopping cart video and you will be able to see how to navigate through the prototype of the shopping cart page. There is two ways to access the shopping cart page. One way is to go to the home page top navigation bar and click on "Cart" button to open up shop and be able to check whats in your purchase list or cart whenever you want to. The second way to view is when you click on the item you want to purchase, you will be sent to the product page and you when click on add to cart, it will add the item to the shopping cart and also take you to the shooping cart page. This addresses the requirement of shopping cart as it allows the user to see what they have purchased before they confirm and make payment of the item for the user's convenience.

The third video is the buy page video and you will be able to see how to navigate through the prototype of the buy page. This addresses the buy page requirement as it allows the user to select the item they want to buy and proceed with the process.

UX research process. Our team decided to focus on semi-structured interview and fictional inquiry interviews for our UX research process. We decided that these two methods would allow us to best find the informations and designs for our website based on what stakeholders believe it will be useful. We perffered this method one 1 on 1 conversation type over group interview like focus group so the stakeholders are comfortable talking about more personal stuff for our questions. Each of our team members conducted a semi-structured or fictional inquiry interview with different stakeholders and we used the information from them to create our codebook and final three requirements.

Anaylsis Process. Our team's analysis process had six steps. Initial axial coding, affinity diagram, codebook, axial coding w/ codebook, consensus building, and requirements generation. We started off by creating an axial coding from the transcript of the interviews we did . We split into two groups and created an affinity diagram based on themes/sub-categories and code we saw in the transcripts, and made a consensus on each of our codebooks. Laslty, we worked on consensus building and requirements generation as a group to anaylze and combine all of our previous steps.

Carlmart - UX Evaluation

UX Evaluation Overview. I perfomred a UX Evaluation on the prototpye of Carlmart design and the results were great for the first ever prototype. I was able to receive a high rating of 4.5/5 from the two senior students of Carleton College who I had give me feedbacks while interacting and testing with my prototype. Both of the students did not have much trouble going through the prototype and praised both my visual and features of the prototype. They pointed out some key changes that should be made which I attempted to imporve immediately. However, the part of the UX Evaluation that did not work out as I expected would be how I conducted the evaluation with both the students at once as that was the UX Evaluation process. I believe that this may have resulted to the point outs and feedback to be similar and repetative as the student that took a look of the prototype heard the first students point and be drawn to them resulting on coming up with similar feedbacks. This made me realize that their are pros and cons on the method of UX Evaluation and it will be crucial for us to conduct several UX Evaluation with variety of methods for diverse feedbacks.

UX Evaluation Process. For our method of UX Evaluation, we chose the think-aloud method where as we had our interviee navigate through our prototype while completing the tasks we assigned them, they had to speak out loud all of their thought process. For example, one of the senior student A who I had him do a task, he was speaking out loud, "okay, I would say cart here, yes, yes, these are all the items. So these three items cost $6 $13". This allowed us to further analyze what the user are looking at, find navigation patterns, and hear there thought process such as what they are expecting the prototype to have and where.

Short description of your UX evaluation tasks. To the two senior students, I had them do three different tasks which includes the testing of the three requirement our decided on Carlmart will need for sure.
Task One: The first task was to navigate to Buy Page by making a purchase of an item. This could be done by clicking on any image of the item that can be found in the home screen of Carlmart. Then the user is navigated to the Buy Page that includes the item description before you put the item in your cart.
Task Two: The second task was to navigate to your cart to check the items that you are currently trying to purchase and allow user to double check what they are buying before they select payment method and confirm their purchase. You can navigate to the cart two ways. One way is to select the item you are trying to buy then click on add to cart which will add the item to the cart and navigate you to your cart to confirm that. The second way is to simply navigate to cart from the home screen which the button is located at the top right in the navigation bar.
Task Three: The third task was to navigate to sell page which allows you to sell the item you want to sell. You can navigate to the sell page by clicking on the sell button at the top right located in the navigation bar. This will take you to a page that is similar to the cart but includes the list of items you are currently selling waiting to be bought by others. In order to navigate to the sell page and sell a new item, you will have to click on the button in the top right.
