Carlmart

Team Members: Colin James, Wesley Yang, Khizar Qureshi

Many students at Carleton College have a problem in the final days of their senior year. T hey find themselves with too many items, necessary for college life, to travel with or have items that they don’t want in their future. Their last days in the dorms are spent throwing lightly used and valuable items in Lighten up, the dumpster, or trying to pawn them off others. Our project will provide a platform that makes it easy for seniors, or Carleton students of any year, to put lightly used and new items onto the site for other Carleton students to buy, barter, or pick up for free.

Prototype addresses the user needs I learned about from the Fictional Inquary I performed with the seniors in Carleton. Both seniors mentioned that a filter feature will be very useful for the user as it will allow them to look for desired items much easier. Therfore I included the filter feature at the left side which allows you to filter the price range of assignments and key items that most students may find useful such as furnitures and electronics. Also one of the senior mentioned that at the home page of the store, it should show recommended items for user to take a look at based off of the usesrs search history and items bought. I also added a feature to like/favorite an item in the store which allows you to keep track of items you want to come back for and possibly implement a recommendation algorithm that keeps them track for the recommendation section at the home page.
